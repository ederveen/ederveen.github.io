Thomas (Tom) H.A. Ederveen, PhD

I am a biomedical senior scientist c.q. bioinformatician at the Radboudumc Nijmegen, currently active in the MBS Computational Biology department, and in strong collaboration with many other disciplines within our institute, most notably the departments of Dermatology and Medical Microbiology. Here, we focus mainly on the analysis of genomes of bacteria, on human and animal microbiota (i.e. microbiome studies) and on bacterial strain tracking. My work further extends to also other types of (multi)omics and molecular data analysis, all mostly in context of biomedical and clinical research.

I see myself as a biologist applying public bioinformatics tools and approaches in order to make sense out of (multi)omics data generated from human samples from various biological sources, often those in the context of a microbiota (microbiomics). Much of my own work relates to the analysis of the human microbiome in both healthy and disease status, in order to identify desirable and undesirable bacteria, respectively. In the end, by mechanistically better understanding human physiology/disease processes, we hope to come up with biomarkers and interventions to better monitor, predict and restore biological processes that explain dysfunction in human disease.

As of 2020, I am also strongly involved in research efforts and development related to FAIR, i.e. to make scientific data Findable Accessible Interoperable and Reusable. Here, I am working on FAIR schema's, data standards and systems such as FAIR data points, in order to make (multi)omics study data available to the world in a human- and machine readable fashion.
